Right now the graphic reads like a concept map, not a business insight.

The issue is two things:

It explains attention instead of proving its value.

The outcomes feel generic rather than strategic.

A healthcare founder, pharma marketer, or clinical leader cares about things like:

adoption

clinician buy-in

patient engagement

differentiation

credibility

alignment

outcomes

So the section needs to feel more like:

Attention drives adoption and impact.

The key shift

Instead of showing things attention leads to, show problems that attention solves.

Decision makers think in barriers, not abstractions.

So the visual becomes:

ATTENTION
   ↓
Breaks through information overload
   ↓
Creates understanding
   ↓
Builds trust
   ↓
Drives adoption
   ↓
Improves outcomes


That tells a clear story of impact.

A stronger concept for the graphic

Instead of a hub, make it a progression.

Scroll animation idea

As the user scrolls:

ATTENTION


Then the chain reveals step by step:

ATTENTION
    ↓
UNDERSTANDING
    ↓
TRUST
    ↓
ADOPTION
    ↓
OUTCOMES


This mirrors the logic of healthcare change.

No one jumps straight to outcomes.

They go through:

attention → understanding → trust → adoption

Why this works better

It feels like a framework, not a list.

Founders love frameworks because they explain how change happens.

It also aligns perfectly with Flint’s narrative:

The Healthcare Attention Gap → The ROI of Attention → The New Standard

What the section headline should emphasize

Instead of just:

The ROI of Attention

Consider:

Attention Drives Adoption in Healthcare

or

Attention Is the Gateway to Adoption

Those lines are much more powerful to a buyer.

The content beneath the graphic could say

Attention is the starting point for every meaningful change in healthcare.

When people pay attention:

complex science becomes understandable

clinicians learn and adopt new approaches

patients build trust in treatment

teams align around a shared vision

And innovation finally gains traction.

Design suggestion

Your current circles are too symmetrical, which makes them feel static.

A vertical cascade would feel more powerful:

ATTENTION
   ↓
UNDERSTANDING
   ↓
TRUST
   ↓
ADOPTION
   ↓
OUTCOMES


Each stage could animate in as the user scrolls.

One more important insight

The moment that should land hardest is adoption.

Because for your ideal clients:

digital health companies need adoption

pharma needs adoption

clinicians need practice change

So the visual could even emphasize it:

ATTENTION
   ↓
UNDERSTANDING
   ↓
TRUST
   ↓
ADOPTION
   ↓
BETTER OUTCOMES


That feels like a business outcome, not a conceptual idea.

The big takeaway

Right now the graphic answers:

What comes from attention?

But what decision makers want to know is:

Why does attention matter to my success?

So the graphic should answer:

Attention is how innovation gets adopted.
